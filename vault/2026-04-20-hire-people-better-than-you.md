# Hire People Better Than You

## Summary
A guide for CEOs and founders on how to hire executives and leaders who are better than you in domains you don't understand. Since you can't assess their domain expertise, the article outlines what signals to look for instead — from whether their ideas excite you, to whether you're already learning from them, to creative reference check techniques that reveal culture fit over credentials.

## Key Ideas
- You **must** hire people better than you at every position — otherwise the org gets bigger, not better
- By 100 people, if the CEO is still the best at anything, they've hired poorly
- A great person isn't 10x better than average — they're **infinitely better** because they generate ideas a hundred average people never would
- You can't evaluate domain expertise you don't have, so evaluate the **person** instead

## Hiring Signals (When You Can't Assess Expertise)

### 1. You leave wanting to implement their ideas
- After the conversation, you're already excited to act on what they said
- Genuine expertise produces specific, actionable ideas tailor-made to your context
- Test: If they went to your competitor instead, would that worry you?

### 2. You're already learning from them
- Zuckerberg's rule: "Only hire people you'd work for" — the real version is: would you *learn* from this person?
- If they've already said something that sent you down rabbit holes during the interview, that's the signal
- Real test: If you took a 6-month sabbatical, would the department be measurably better when you returned?

### 3. They elevate the whole organization
- Great leaders improve more than their silo — people issues, communication, decision-making, goal-setting, org structure
- Test for this by asking about real leadership challenges you're facing today

### 4. Ask them to solve a real problem you face
- Give actual company challenges; watch how they think, not the specific answer
- Look for: incisive clarifying questions, acknowledgment of what they don't know, real experience (not parroted frameworks)

### 5. Reference checks for culture fit, not expertise
- Ask: "Construct the perfect scenario where this person absolutely kills it"
- Ask the inverse: "Construct the scenario where they die inside and mess everything up"
- Ask: "What strengths are they unaware of because they come so naturally?"
- These reveal whether the person will thrive in **your** specific environment

## On Mis-Hires
- False negatives (rejecting good people) cost a continued search
- False positives (hiring wrong people) hurt the team, delay progress, and are terrible for the person hired
- Everyone on the team knows about a mis-hire before you do — act immediately, you're already three months late
- If your best people are complaining instead of being inspired, you hired incorrectly

## Insights & Claims
- Delegation alone produces a team that's never better than the founder — you must hire *up*
- You will never have the counterfactual on whether a different hire would've done better
- What you can know is whether they changed how you see your own company, found problems you didn't know existed, and made people around them sharper
- The interview is about the person, not the details of their domain

## Actionable Takeaways
- Stop trying to evaluate domain expertise you don't have — evaluate the person
- Use real company problems in interviews to watch how candidates think
- Restructure reference checks around environment fit, not endorsements
- Act fast on mis-hires — lingering is the most expensive mistake
- Look for people whose ideas make you excited, not just impressed
