# Trip Planning Agent That Can Self-Grow

## Summary
An idea for an AI agent focused on trip planning that improves itself over time — learning from past trips, user preferences, and new data to become a better planner with each use.

The concept combines autonomous travel planning (destinations, itineraries, budgets, logistics) with a self-improving loop. The agent would accumulate knowledge from every interaction — what worked, what didn't, which recommendations landed — and use that to sharpen future suggestions. Over time it evolves from a generic planner into a deeply personalized travel companion that understands Yash's travel style.

## Key Ideas
- An AI agent dedicated to end-to-end trip planning
- Self-growth: the agent learns and improves autonomously over time
- Could accumulate preference data, destination knowledge, and planning patterns
- Growth could mean expanding its own toolset, data sources, or reasoning strategies

## Insights & Claims
- Static trip planners hit a ceiling — a self-growing agent breaks past that by adapting
- The "self-grow" aspect implies some form of memory, reflection, or self-modification
- This sits at the intersection of personal AI assistants and autonomous agents

## Actionable Takeaways
- Sketch out what "self-grow" means concretely: memory? tool acquisition? fine-tuning?
- Define the MVP: what's the simplest version that still demonstrates growth?
- Explore existing trip planning APIs and data sources it could plug into
- Consider how it stores and retrieves learnings between sessions

## Why This Matters
- Personal agents that improve over time are a high-leverage idea — trip planning is a great domain because preferences are rich and repeatable.

## Applications
- A personal travel assistant that gets better with every trip
- Could extend the pattern to other domains (meal planning, fitness, learning paths)
- Potential open-source project or product

## Questions
- What does "self-grow" look like technically — memory, tool creation, or something else?
- Should it grow from explicit feedback, implicit signals, or both?
- What's the first trip you'd test it on?
